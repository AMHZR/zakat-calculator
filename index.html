Learn about zakat

Zakat is an act of worship on qualifying wealth.

It is one of the five pillars of Islam. Broadly, it applies when a Muslim owns qualifying wealth above the nisab threshold for one lunar year, then gives a fixed share to eligible recipients for the sake of Allah.

What zakat means

Zakat is not a general donation. It is an obligation on specific forms of wealth, calculated with clear rules around nisab, hawl, and the types of assets that are included.

Why Muslims pay it

Muslims pay zakat in obedience to Allah, to purify wealth, and to fulfill the rights of people in need. It is both a spiritual duty and a social responsibility.

Who usually pays

In practical terms, zakat becomes due when qualifying net wealth stays at or above the nisab for a lunar year. This calculator focuses on the common monetary zakat case for personal wealth.

What this app helps with

This calculator estimates zakat on cash, bank balances, receivables, gold, silver, investments, business inventory, and short-term liabilities. It is built for clarity, not for complex fatwa decisions.

Benefits and wisdom

  • It helps purify wealth and reminds the believer that provision comes from Allah.
  • It supports people who have a right over wealth and keeps social care active in the community.
  • It builds discipline, gratitude, and a more honest relationship with money.
  • It gives the believer a chance to seek reward from Allah through a pillar of Islam.

Important reminder

This app is a practical calculator, not a fatwa. For business complexity, retirement accounts, local fiqh differences, or edge cases, confirm the treatment with a qualified scholar.
